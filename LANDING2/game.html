// THE GAME

You weren't supposed to be alone.

A 2D space survival story. No weapons. No HUD. One AI talks to you. Sixteen chapters from the wreckage to the gateway of light.

// COLD OPEN

The first image of the game is debris.

No briefing. No tutorial. No HUD. The rescue shuttle activated because something destroyed the ship you were on, and you happened to be close enough to one of the lifeboats.

That's the story. You get it in one image, in silence, with nothing on screen but the slow rotation of the wreckage you used to call home. Every module you'll later pick up was dropped here, by people who came before you. They didn't make it out either.

The rest you discover by surviving long enough to see it.

// THE SHUTTLE

A magenta triangle in a galaxy of blue.

The shuttle is called Galaxtarus. She has no cannons. No turrets. No weapons of any kind.

She was built for one thing — keeping you alive in places where nothing else survives. Her color is deliberate. In this cold blue universe, she stands out. She's visible. She's fragile. She's alone.

You are the pilot. You have no name. You don't speak. The shuttle does what you tell her. Her inertia is real. Her engine over-heats if you push it. Every choice you make leaves a trail in the dark.

In this entire galaxy, exactly one voice speaks. His name is L.O.S.
// THE AI

He took care of the garden. He still takes care of someone.

L.O.S. was the gardener of the mothership's botanical bay. The garden is gone with the rest. He's the only voice left in the universe.

He's damaged. He glitches mid-sentence. He grows weary. He cracks a joke right when you needed one. He addresses you like an old friend who never asked permission to be familiar — and the game never explains why. You'll feel it before you can name it.

And then there's the part most studios would hide. His every line was written by an actual AI. We didn't think the AI in the game should sound human-written. We let one write him. Honest, on the box, on the page. The AI in the game is written by an AI.

— tagline : “Lonely. Together.”

// THE LOOP

You can't fight. You can outlast.

There is no enemy you defeat in Galaxtarus. Only ones you survive long enough to leave behind.

You drift. You dodge. You read the spaces between asteroids. You bait predators into burning their reserves. You scavenge modules from those who came before — and went under. Every shield, every burst, every save-point was theirs first.

The shuttle starts crippled. The boost is locked. The tools are scattered. Each chapter unlocks one more piece of what Galaxtarus was supposed to be — until the moment you face the thing that's been hunting you the whole time.

// HAVOC

Six legs. A diamond eye. No mercy. No malice.

The thing that guards the way out is yellow crystal. Angular. Deliberate. You don't fight it — you're a rescue shuttle. You run.

HAVOC is not evil. It's territorial. Whatever lies in the deep zone — the ancient monoliths, the pulsing star, the gateway of light — it doesn't want you near it. The eye charges. The eye fires. The eye recharges. You learn its rhythm, or you don't get to see the other side.
